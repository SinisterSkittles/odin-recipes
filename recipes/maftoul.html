Maftoul

Description

Maftoul is a traditional Palestinian dish made from pearl-shaped wheat, often compared to couscous. It’s typically prepared with chickpeas, onions, garlic, and a blend of aromatic spices. However, Maftoul is a versatile dish—while the classic version uses chicken broth, it can easily be made vegetarian by substituting with a rich tomato broth.

You can enjoy Maftoul in its simplest form or get creative by adding your own twist with additional vegetables or seasonings. Whether you choose to make it from scratch or buy it premade from the store, this dish is a flavorful, hearty meal that’s sure to satisfy.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Onion
  • Olive Oil
  • Maftoul (Palestinian Couscous)
  • Chickpeas
  • Cauliflower
  • Chicken Broth or Tomato Broth
  • Butter
  • Lemon
  • Garlic
  • Parsley

Steps

  1. Prepare the spice mix: add all the spices to a bowl and mix to combine.
  2. Bake the chicken: preheat the oven and season the chicken. Bake with the onions for about 40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked.
  3. Boil the maftoul: while the chicken is in the oven, cook the couscous in chicken broth for about 20-30 minutes, or until soft
  4. Prepare the chickpeas: fry the garlic and remaining spice blend, then add the chickpeas with some broth and simmer for a few minutes.
  5. Assemble: combine the cooked maftoul with the chickpea mixture, adding a splash of liquid if needed so that it's not clumping.
  6. Serve: plate the chickpea mixture and top with the roasted chicken and onions. Garnish with parsley and lemon, and serve.
